Catan: Explorers & Pirates — Rules Reference
Overview
5 scenarios played in sequence. Each adds mechanics. All scenarios use the same base rules below.
Base Rule Differences from Classic Catan
No development cards
No cities (settlements only)
No Longest Road or Largest Army cards
No robber tile (Scenario 2+ introduces a pirate ship instead)
No harbours — universal 3:1 bank trade
7 rolled: players with 8+ cards still discard half, but no robber moves
Gold compensation: if your roll produces no resources for you, take 1 gold from supply
Settlements cost the same as Classic but are called harbor settlements when built on new islands
Components (per player)
5 settlements (from base game)
15 roads (from base game)
4 harbor settlements (expansion piece — worth 2 VP each)
3 ships
2 settlers
2 starting gold coins
Gold
Tracked separately from resources — not subject to 7-discard, not stealable
2 gold → any 1 resource (can do this twice per turn)
Earned via: discovery reward, gold field hexes (Scenario 2+), gold compensation on blank rolls
Can be traded with other players like a resource
Ships
Building
Cost: 1 Lumber + 1 Wool
Place adjacent to one of your harbor settlements
Cannot place on an edge adjacent to an undiscovered hex (would immediately discover it)
If all 3 ships are on the board, remove one to rebuild elsewhere (pieces in hold are lost)
Moving
Move ships during the Movement Phase — after Trade & Build, before turn ends
Each ship has 4 movement points per turn
Moving one edge costs 1 movement point
Can change direction mid-move
Must finish one ship's movement before starting another
Can pass through edges occupied by other ships
Cannot end movement on an edge already holding 2 ships
Max 2 ships per edge (any player combination)
Spend 1 Wool to buy 2 extra movement points for one ship (once per ship per turn)
Hold
Each ship has a hold: 1 large piece (settler, fish haul) OR 2 small pieces (crew, spice sack)
Loading/unloading costs no movement points
Can load/unload mid-move
Sea Routes
Ships move along sea routes — edges that border at least one sea hex
Coastal edges (between land and sea) are valid sea routes
Settlers
Loading
Pick up a settler from your supply during Movement Phase — load it into a ship's hold
Settler ships (ship + settler in hold) are the key exploration tool
Building a Harbor Settlement
Move a settler ship to a coastal vertex on a new island
Unload the settler → place a harbor settlement at that vertex
Costs nothing beyond the settler piece
Harbor settlements = 2 VP (vs 1 VP for regular settlements)
You also get 1 bonus VP for being the first player to build on a new island
Discovering New Hexes
When a ship moves so that one of its endpoints (bow or stern) points at an undiscovered hex corner, that hex is immediately revealed
Flip it face-up, place its number token
The discovering player receives 1 gold as a reward
Discovery ends that ship's movement for the turn
Scenario 1 — Land Ho! (introductory)
VP target: 8 VP
No pirate ship, no crews, no missions
Win by building harbor settlements on new islands
Starting setup: each player gets 1 harbor settlement + 1 settler ship (ship with settler loaded) + 1 settlement + 1 road, placed at fixed positions
Scenario 2 — Pirate Lairs
VP target: 9 VP
Adds: crews, pirate ship, pirate lairs, gold fields
Crews are small pieces loaded into ships (cost: 1 Wool + 1 Ore)
Attack a pirate lair by moving a ship with a crew to an adjacent edge
If your crew count > lair's strength → lair captured, becomes gold field, you earn VP
On a 7: active player moves the neutral pirate ship 1 edge; it steals 1 resource from any player with a ship on an adjacent edge
Scenario 3 — Fish for Catan
VP target: 10 VP
Adds: fish shoal hexes, fish hauls, fish delivery mission
Fish shoal hexes produce fish hauls when their number is rolled
Load a fish haul into a ship's hold, deliver it to the Council island → earn VP + gold
Scenario 4 — Spices for Catan
VP target: 11 VP
Adds: spice hexes, spice sacks, spice delivery mission
Spice hexes grant special advantages when you build on them:
Fast Gold — earn 2 gold instead of 1 on discovery
Swift Voyage — one ship gets +2 movement for free each turn
Pirate Bonus — your ships ignore the pirate ship
Scenario 5 — Explorers & Pirates (full game)
VP target: 13 VP
All mechanics from scenarios 1–4 active simultaneously
All 3 mission types active
